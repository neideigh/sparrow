my journey

A timeline of my journey with computer science
2014-2020

'14

washington state university

While working in the department of integrative physiology and neuroscience as an undergraduate research assistant I was introduced to Python for the first time by a post-doctoral student also working in the lab. He pointed out to me as practicing neuroscientists, we have a need for percise image recognition software as it allows us to see newly formed protein structures in neurons that change and develop in response to artificial stimuli. By leveraging different "edge-detection" algorithms, python finds the edges of newly formed protein structures to aid research and development for brain-based diseases.

'16

ecommerce & fba

I began working for a tech company that sold premium brands product's on the Amazon Marketplace in the summer of 2016. My challenge each day as a member of the "studio" department on the content team, was to ensure our partnered brands product listing pages were optimized to the greatest extent for Amazon to achieve the highest page rank for our desired keywords. I implemented Python to automate detail page-monitoring for 15,000 detail pages to ensure validity and accuracy on behalf of our brands. In combination with intense marketing efforts, we saw a number of these products achieve the #1 selling item badge awarded by Amazon. This job experience taught me the meaning of a quality user experience as customer confidence improves with seamless design and appropriate product positioning.
